# Black hole

This project was an exercise born from a desire to learn about Kepler's and Newton's Laws of motion in the context of astrodynamics. The visual appearance of a non-rotating/Schwarzschild black hole is approximated with the Two-Body equation and its underlying assumptions, with the black hole as the central body and light as massless particles. This way, photons travel in orbits around the black hole, described with conic sections, and orbital elements are manipulated to find relevant information.

Although this method misses the mark on some visual characteristics of black holes, it is quite simple in computation compared to another, much more common, method of numerically integrating the path of light.
